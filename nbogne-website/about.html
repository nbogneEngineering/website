About nBogne

"nBogne" means "they have received it" in Twi. It's the confirmation that data arrived where it needed to go.

Our Story

Built from real experience

In 2024, our co-founder walked into a clinic with a referral. Both hospitals had working EMRs. The doctor called by hand to ask for his test results.

Different systems. Same problem. This happens every day across Africa. Doctors order duplicate tests. Patients pay twice. Healthcare workers waste hours chasing records.

We built nBogne because Africa deserves healthcare infrastructure that actually works.

Our Mission

To make health records follow patients across Africa — instantly, securely, and without requiring anyone to trust a central database.

Our Vision

To become the default infrastructure for moving sensitive data across systems — starting with healthcare, then expanding to any domain where trust, compliance, and interoperability matter.

Why Stateless?

Trust built into architecture

Ghana's LIMS was cancelled in 2024. Not because the technology didn't work — because hospitals didn't trust centralized storage with patient data.

They were right to be cautious. The interoperability market has been asking hospitals to surrender data custody. We did the opposite.

If the system can't store patient data, it can't betray it.

The nBogne Principle

"No technology can fix a trust problem. But architecture can. When you can't store data, you can't betray it."
